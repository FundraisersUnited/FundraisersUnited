PFRA Compliance

Our commitment to ethical fundraising standards

Last Updated: May 1, 2025

Fundraisers United is a proud member of the Public Fundraising Regulatory Association (PFRA), the self-regulatory body for face-to-face fundraising in Australia. This page outlines our commitment to the PFRA's standards and our approach to ethical fundraising practices.

PFRA Membership Statement

Fundraisers United is a registered member of the PFRA Australia. Our membership number is FU2025-001. We adhere to all PFRA standards, guidelines, and codes of practice in our fundraising operations.

Our Commitment to the PFRA Standards

As a PFRA member, we are committed to upholding the highest standards of professional conduct in all our fundraising activities. This includes:

  • Ensuring all our fundraisers are properly trained and informed about the PFRA's standards
  • Following all site management rules and location agreements
  • Implementing proper identification and transparency protocols
  • Maintaining respectful interactions with the public
  • Providing accurate information about the charities we represent
  • Protecting the public's personal and financial information
  • Participating in regular PFRA compliance checks and audits

Fundraiser Identification

All Fundraisers United team members operating in the field:

  • Wear clearly visible identification badges showing their name, photo, and the charity they are representing
  • Wear branded clothing identifying them as professional fundraisers
  • Clearly communicate that they are paid fundraisers working on behalf of the charity
  • Can provide official paperwork confirming their authorization to fundraise for the charity

Fundraiser Training

Our comprehensive training program ensures all fundraisers:

  • Understand and comply with the PFRA Standards and relevant codes of practice
  • Are knowledgeable about the charities they represent
  • Follow ethical fundraising practices and respect potential donors' decisions
  • Know how to properly engage with vulnerable persons
  • Understand privacy regulations and data protection requirements
  • Are aware of complaint handling procedures

Site Management

We maintain strict adherence to PFRA site management protocols:

  • Only operating in authorized locations with proper permits
  • Respecting site rotation schedules and location agreements
  • Maintaining appropriate distance from entry/exit points, ATMs, and other sensitive areas
  • Limiting team sizes according to PFRA guidelines
  • Ensuring fundraisers do not obstruct pedestrian traffic
  • Promptly responding to any site-related concerns or complaints

Donor Protection

We are committed to donor protection through:

  • Clear disclosure of fundraiser status and charity affiliation
  • Transparent information about how donations will be used
  • Secure handling of personal and financial information
  • No-pressure fundraising approach
  • Proper opt-out and cancellation procedures
  • Special protocols for vulnerable persons

Complaint Resolution

We take complaints seriously and have established procedures for resolving them:

  • All complaints are logged and reviewed by management
  • Complaints are addressed promptly, typically within 48 hours
  • Serious complaints are reported to the relevant charity partner and the PFRA
  • Continuous improvement measures are implemented based on complaint feedback
  • Annual review of complaint patterns to identify and address systemic issues

To file a complaint about our fundraising practices, please contact us at complaints@fundraisersunited.com.au or call +61 2 1234 5679.

PFRA Verification

To verify our PFRA membership or to learn more about the PFRA's standards, you can visit the PFRA website or contact them directly.
